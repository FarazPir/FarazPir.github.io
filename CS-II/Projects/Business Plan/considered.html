Considered Proposals

Thefit

Proposal Page

People Involved

  • Jana Daouk
  • Zaenb Al Ameri

Overview

Thefit is an outfit generator that uses multiple factors such as body type, skin tone, trends, and modesty preferences to generate an outfit for any occasion. By doing this it saves the user the time and stress of having to choose their own outfit. I considered it because it seems like a fun and simple idea, and it effectively solves a real issue for a lot of people. Figuring out the algorithm to generate the outfit could be a challenge, but it would be very doable.

Verdict

I decided to reject this proposal because it's just too basic. There’s a lot of potential for ISPs and I feel like this app doesn’t fully utilize it. I think there are more cool ideas besides simply making an app to generate outfits, ideas that have more features and things involved. For the most part this just consists of basic coding for an app and one complicated algorithm. Overall I think there are better, more interesting proposals.

RFID IDs

Proposal Page

People Involved

  • Dylan Pettijohn
  • Brayden Edington
  • Sahil Patil
  • Srisaivikas Munagala

Overview

This idea proposes that all students and faculty at a school have an RFID label attached to their ID, and that there will be a scanner at all the entrance doors to keep the school secure and make sure only people with their IDs come in the school. I considered this idea because I think working with RFID labels is a cool idea and I like that it can be applied to our school.

Verdict

However I’m going to have to reject this idea because I simply don’t agree with it. I don’t think RFID scanners are really necessary when the same goal can be accomplished with NFC or literally just traditional barcodes. The idea feels unnecessary and I don’t think it would be something a school would want to have. The code also would most likely be really easy, so I think I’d have to turn this one down.

Proposal Page

People Involved

  • Onik Hoque
  • Pranav Krishnan

Overview

I felt like I could still find another idea that had a greater appeal to me and was still doable and involved some new learning.

unIQue

Proposal Page

People Involved

  • Mason Graham

Overview

This idea is an interactive experience that helps raise IQ, critical thinking skills, brain strength, and aid in mental health. It does this through engaging games, healthy lifestyle suggestions, and proven scientific proverbs. I strongly considered this one because the idea was unique and I feel like it had a lot to offer since it has so many different components. The coding process also would be interesting because while some parts would be simple, the games will be more complex and will likely require some learning of javascript or other programming languages.

Verdict

Ultimately, this was the proposal I decided to land on because of its concepts and its complex, but not too complicated idea. Its coding will be a decent challenge and be a fun learning experience while not being too impossible. Its concept is also one the more unique ones, so I think our final-product will be something unique compared to the other ideas. Also the chatbot from the previous proposal can be incorporated into this to help in the mental health aspect so I think overall this proposal has the most going for it.
